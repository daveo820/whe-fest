AW
Health

Dr. Amanda Wells

Integrative Medicine Pioneer

Board-certified physician and author of The Healing Body, Dr. Wells has redefined how mainstream medicine approaches women's hormonal health and nutrition.

Dr. Amanda Wells has spent over 20 years bridging the gap between conventional medicine and holistic healing practices. As founder of the Integrative Wellness Institute, she has treated thousands of women suffering from conditions long dismissed by traditional practitioners — from chronic fatigue to autoimmune disease to the invisible burden of hormonal imbalance.

At WHE Fest, Dr. Wells will lead the session "Food as Medicine: Reclaiming the American Kitchen," where she'll outline actionable strategies every woman can use immediately to reclaim her health.

SB
Health

Dr. Sarah Brennan

Toxicology & Women's Wellness Expert

A leading voice on environmental toxins and their disproportionate impact on women's health, Dr. Brennan translates complex science into empowering action.

Dr. Sarah Brennan has published over 60 peer-reviewed papers on environmental endocrine disruptors, hormonal toxicity, and the long-term health consequences of modern agricultural practices on women's bodies. She serves on three national health advisory boards and is a frequent congressional witness on chemical policy reform.

Her WHE Fest session "Hormones, Toxins & the Modern Woman" is one of the most anticipated panels of the festival.

PT
Health

Patricia Thomas

Founder, Clean Food Coalition

Patricia turned her family's health crisis into a national movement, building one of the country's fastest-growing coalitions advocating for clean, transparent food labeling.

After her daughter's diagnosis with a condition linked to food additives, Patricia Thomas left a successful corporate career to become one of the most effective food policy advocates in the country. Her organization now has chapters in 42 states and has influenced landmark labeling legislation in several key markets.

RC
Policy

Rep. Rachel Collins

Health Policy Champion

A two-term congresswoman and former nurse, Rep. Collins is one of Washington's most trusted voices on women's health legislation and healthcare transparency.

Representative Rachel Collins brought a stethoscope and a mission to Capitol Hill. A former emergency room nurse and public health administrator, she has authored landmark bills on maternal health, health data privacy, and pharmaceutical pricing transparency. Her session "Advocating for Health Sovereignty in Washington" will equip every attendee with tools to engage their own representatives.

JH
Policy

Judge Jennifer Holt

Constitutional Health Rights Attorney

A constitutional attorney and former federal judge, Jennifer has become the country's foremost legal voice on bodily autonomy, medical freedom, and health privacy rights.

Jennifer Holt spent 12 years on the federal bench before returning to advocacy, where she now leads a nonprofit legal defense fund supporting families and practitioners who are fighting to protect medical freedom. Her plain-spoken ability to translate legal complexity into citizen action has made her a sought-after voice at national gatherings.

ML
Empowerment

Maria Lopez

Founder, Moms Health Network

Maria built the Moms Health Network from a Facebook group of 12 women into a coalition of over 800,000 mothers demanding systemic change in how America feeds and medicalizes its children.

Maria Lopez is proof that grassroots can become great. What started as a neighborhood conversation about school lunches became one of the most influential parent-led health advocacy networks in the country. A mother of four, speaker, and organizer, Maria brings both fire and strategy to every room she enters. Her WHE Fest session "Community Leadership Roundtable" is an interactive workshop designed to turn attendees into leaders in their own communities.

TK
Empowerment

Tanya King

Author & Women's Leadership Coach

Bestselling author of The Sovereign Woman and executive coach to hundreds of women in leadership, Tanya helps women trust their instincts and lead from a place of authentic power.

Tanya King has coached CEOs, senators, surgeons, and school board members — all women who came to her feeling like impostors in their own power. Her work sits at the intersection of women's psychology, leadership development, and the deeply personal work of learning to trust yourself. Her session "Building Movements: From Community to Capitol" will be one of the festival's most powerful afternoons.

DW
Nonprofit Leaders

Diane Whitmore

CEO, National Women's Health Alliance

Diane leads one of the country's largest women's health nonprofits, overseeing programs that serve over 2 million women annually across 50 states.

Diane Whitmore brings 30 years of nonprofit leadership to WHE Fest, including roles at HHS, the Gates Foundation, and as founder of two successful national health initiatives. Her pragmatic approach to building sustainable organizations has made her a mentor to a generation of women leaders in the health and wellness space.

NR
Nonprofit Leaders

Nina Reyes

Executive Director, Moms Across America

A tireless advocate for clean food and transparent government, Nina has led Moms Across America's national expansion and its growing influence in health policy circles.

Nina Reyes joined Moms Across America as a volunteer, and within five years became its executive director. Under her leadership, the organization has doubled its reach, launched a legislative advocacy program, and formed partnerships with dozens of aligned health organizations nationwide. Her connection to WHE Fest reflects the natural partnership between two movements building the same future.
